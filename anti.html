Before we begin nonviolent communication, let's review the definition of "observation" and "feeling".

From the three flipcards below, find the only "observation"

"My baby does not respond to me immediately."
Not observation
"My baby starts crying from 1am to 2am."
Congratulations! This is the real observation!
"My baby alwayscries at night and I cannot sleep."
Not observation

From the three flipcards below, find the only "feeling"

"I am not a good mother."
Not feeling
This is your judgement.
"I don't think my baby likes me."
Not feeling
Still judgement
"The way that my baby cries makes me feel upset."
Congratulations! This is the only feeling!

Sometimes, we would mix our subjective judgement with our feelings and observation,

which gets us stuck in negative emotions.

We hope you can feel better by distinguishing the real "observation" and "feeling", and stop judging yourself.

STEP1: Observation


"Nonviolent communication emphasizes the distinction between observation and comment."



STEP2: Feeling


"We need to be able to express our feelings without repressing them."



STEP3: Needs


"Feelings are rooted in our own needs and expectations, as well as our perceptions of other people's behaviors."



STEP4: Request


"If we organize our needs and make requests, others are more likely to respond positively."
